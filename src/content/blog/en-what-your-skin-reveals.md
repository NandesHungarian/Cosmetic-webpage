Your skin is not just a wrapper — it communicates constantly. Dryness, where breakouts appear, pigmentation patterns: these are all signals worth interpreting.

## Face Zones as a Map

In traditional facial therapy, different areas of the face are linked to different organ systems. This is not a diagnostic tool, but it can serve as a useful guide.

- **Forehead**: digestion, gut flora
- **Nose bridge and cheeks**: respiratory system, blood pressure
- **Chin and area around the mouth**: hormonal balance

## What Dry Skin Signals

The skin’s natural hydration depends on the integrity of its lipid barrier. When the barrier is compromised — by harsh cleansers, hot water, or incompatible ingredients — water evaporates from the skin. This is called transepidermal water loss (TEWL).

The solution is not always more moisturiser. Often the answer is simplifying your routine and focusing on barrier-restoring ingredients: ceramides, niacinamide, panthenol.

## When to See a Professional

If your skin has been showing the same issue for months despite home care, that is a sign the cause runs deeper. A personalised skin analysis will reveal exactly where it makes sense to intervene.
