Skincare isn't complicated — it just requires consistent attention. Most people come to me when they're already struggling with a problem: breakouts, dryness, or the first signs of ageing. Yet prevention is far simpler and less costly than treatment.

## The foundation: cleansing

The root of most skin problems is improper facial cleansing. During the day, pollution, dust and make-up residue accumulate on the skin — these need to be removed in the evening. But it's equally important not to wash your face with very hot water or use aggressive soap, as this disrupts the skin's natural protective barrier.

Recommended routine:
- Morning: gentle gel or micellar water
- Evening: double cleanse — first an oil-based cleanser, then a foaming gel

## Hydration: everyone's needs are different

Many people think oily skin doesn't need moisturiser. This is a misconception. Oily skin can also be dehydrated — in this case, sebum production increases because the skin is trying to compensate for the missing moisture. The solution is a light, non-comedogenic moisturiser.

For dry skin, richer creams containing ceramides help. For sensitive skin, fragrance- and alcohol-free formulas are the safest choice.

## Sun protection: the only "anti-ageing" that actually works

If you introduce just one thing into your routine, make it daily SPF. UV radiation is responsible for approximately 80% of premature ageing — this is scientifically proven. In winter too. On cloudy days too. SPF 30 is the minimum, but in the city SPF 50 is the safer choice.

If a problem persists despite a consistent home routine, it's worth getting a consultation. A skin analysis helps map exactly what skin type and condition you're dealing with — and from that, a truly personalised solution can be found.
